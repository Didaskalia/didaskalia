Memorandum of Understanding between the Open University and Didaskalia

Preamble
The Open University and Didaskalia recognise the close correspondences and overlap of interests between a number of their research, pedagogical and online activities relating to the reception of ancient drama. At present these activities are carried out independently. They agree that a new collaboration would offer valuable opportunities significantly to consolidate and expand the range and quality of each others' activities in certain areas. In particular, collaboration would assist the Classical Studies Department's Research project on the reception of classical texts and Didaskalia in linking high-quality research materials with high-profile, international scholarly and pedagogical outreach, and to foster exchange and growth of knowledge among students, researchers, academics, and theatre practitioners. Collaboration would offer Didaskalia a secure foundation for sustainable growth. The Reception of Classical Texts Research Project and Didaskalia would both be able to draw on each other's strengths in preparing and carrying out gmrant-funded research and teaching innovations, such as the JISC-funded ARCHES project (£150k) in which Didaskalia is a currently participant, and the current and future projects of the Open University researchers. The Reception of Classical Texts Research Project and Didaskalia have therefore drawn up this Memorandum of Understanding.

Didaskalia is an international electronic journal for research in Greek and Roman drama and its reception and is the world leader in its field. In addition to publishing refereed articles and Special Editions, it contains Reviews, performance Listings, VR representations of the material environment of ancient theatre, an agora/forum area for discussion and a Study area. It is available freely on-line, currently at http://www.didaskalia.net.


Association of Didaskalia and The Open University
1 From June 2003, or as soon as possible thereafter, Didaskalia will be published in association with the Open University.

1.1 From June 2003, or as soon as possible thereafter, Didaskalia will carry the Open University logo on its home page and elsewhere within the web-site as appropriate. It will also carry statements in appropriate locations within the website giving full recognition to the nature of the relationship with the OU.

1.2 The Open University and Didaskalia will proactively work together to enhance the range and quality of each other's educational and scholarly resources, activities and profiles in concrete, demonstrable ways. The Editorial Board of Didaskalia will continue to have full academic independence in respect of publication of articles, special issues etc. The existing conventions for independent refereeing of articles submitted will continue to apply.

Representation
2.0 Didaskalia is managed and directed by the Editor in consultation with the Editorial Board. The Editorial Board will normally include an Associate Director, a Listings Editor (contributed by the American Philological Association Committee on Ancient and Modern Performance), a Reviews Editor, an Education Editor, and such other Sub-Editors as the Editor may from time to time appoint.

2.1 From June 2003, the Editorial Board of Didaskalia will also include an Academic Representative nominated by the OU Department of Classics. In the first instance, this person will be Professor Lorna Hardwick. The OU representative should be an ex officio member of the Board. The Education Editor should be appointed in consultation with the OU representative.

2.2 The Editor and the OU Academic Representative in consultation with the Editorial Board will agree a Development Plan, to be reviewed and updated annually. The Development Plan will outline the duties of the Administrator, Web-Editor and such additional Web-Development as may be agreed, taking into consideration the requirements of collaborative projects between the Open University, Didaskalia and/or other partners.

Technical Infrastructure
3.0 From June 2003, or as soon thereafter as practicable, Didaskalia will be transferred to an Open University server, and relocated to a URL within the OU domain, e.g. www.didaskalia.open.ac.uk.

3.1 The OU will undertake to give reasonable cooperation and necessary technical access during the period of handover to the Didaskalia server to any persons nominated, and named in advance, by the Editor to carry out web-editing, web-development or other work as determined by the Editor.

3.2 The OU will undertake to give reasonable cooperation and necessary technical access to external bodies nominated by the Editor and approved by the OU, in order to carry out, for example, grant-funded projects, the preparation of study resources, the creation / modification of interactive resources, or new issues of the Journal, subject to the staffing resources and infrastructure set out in 4 below.

Staffing and Management Infrastructure
4.0 From October 2003, the Open University will assume the cost of routine web-editing and web-development and occasional web-development as defined under Roles and Responsibilities below to carry out the programme of work devised in consultation with the OU Academic Representative in the annual Development Plan (2.3 above).

4.1 For an interim period of approximately one year (until October 2003), web-editing will primarily be carried out, as at present, by a nominee of the Editor, externally to the OU, and funded to a maximum of £1,200 by the University of Warwick. In the course of 2003, this person will gradually be phased out as a person appointed by the OU is phased in.

4.2 From October 2003, the OU will provide staff for routine web-editing and day-to-day administration of Didaskalia, amounting to a total of 0.2 FTE. (These two roles may be assumed by the same person.) The Web-editor and Administrator, who will usually be based in the OU Reception of Classical Texts Research Project, will be managed by the OU Academic Representative or the Director of Reception of Classical Texts Research Project (if he/she is not the OU Academic Representative).

4.3 Didaskalia may participate in grant-funded, collaborative projects, such as the JISC-funded ARCHES project (October 2002 - September 2004) necessitating web-editing, administration, and web-development to be undertaken by partners external to the Open University but channelled through the OU web admin. Didaskalia will contribute to such projects "in association with the Open University" and the Open University will be given full credit for its participation through Didaskalia. The Open University will not be responsible for providing additional funding for any work generated by the JISC-funded ARCHES project.

Roles and Responsibilities
5.0 The Editor is responsible for directing the planning, management, content, presentation, and development of Didaskalia. The Editor's duties include: overall planning and scheduling of the work of Didaskalia in consultation with the Editorial Board and through the annual Development Plan agreed with the OU Academic Representative; appointment of guest editors, sub-editors and other personnel except those who, under the terms of this Memorandum are to be appointed by the Open University; liaison with the OU Academic Representative or person nominated by her / him; routine liaison with the Administrator and Web-Editor in order to implement the Development Plan and other minor works that from time to time may arise; liaison with technical staff or their managers appointed to carry out web-development work; preparing and/or co-ordinating (joint) grant applications designed to enhance or further the research, publishing and pedagogical missions of Didaskalia and wherever possible in ways designed to further the Open University's research and pedagogical interests; liaison with research partners in joint-projects, including grant-funded projects; propose and manage technical and other initiatives; control expenditure against Didaskalia's budget in accordance with the priorities and work agreed in the annual Development Plan; commissioning and editing contributions to the Journal and other parts of the web-site. The OU appointed web administrator will be the Budget holder and budgeting will follow OU protocols.

5.1 Routine web-editing is a non-academic, technical role encompassing the maintenance of up-to-date content on the web-site, the preparation of new content, including new issues of the Journal, for publication. It may include digitising analogue resources (e.g. still or moving images and audio recordings) as well as documenting the digital resources and publishing them online. It may include making transcriptions of audio recordings of interviews, panel discussions, or lectures. It may encompass developing online study resources and undertaking new initiatives where these do not require technical skills significantly in advance of those required routinely to maintain and update the web-site. Routine web-editing may encompass other related duties subject to the agreement of both the OU Academic Representative and the Editor. The Editor may from time to time to nominate or commission other persons to carry out any aspect of these or additional duties (e.g. in the case of grant-funded projects), and the Web-Editor Administrator will be expected to cooperate fully with these arrangements subject to limitation of time set out in 4.2 above and management arrangements set out in 5.0 above.

5.2 Day-to-day administration is a non-academic, administrative role encompassing liaising with the Editor and OU Academic Representative to define priorities, draw up schedules of work, and report regularly on progress. It may encompass corresponding with third parties (e.g. contributors, sub-editors, search engines, newsgroups, related publications, copyright holders). The administrator may be asked to assemble and prepare documentation to support claims upon the project budget. The Administrator's responsibilities may encompass other related duties subject to the agreement of both the OU Academic Representative and the Editor. The Editor may from time to time to nominate or commission other persons to carry out any aspect of these or additional duties (e.g. in the case of grant-funded projects) and the Administrator will be expected to cooperate fully with these arrangements - subject to limitation of time set out in 4.2. above and management arrangements set out in 5.0 above.

5.3 Occasional web-development encompasses medium- or major-scale technical tasks beyond the scope of routine web-editing as outlined in 5.1 above. Such tasks might include creating or publishing databases, adding extra functionality to the web-site, or creating interfaces within Didaskalia enabling users to query other online resources. Such initiatives to be included in the annual development plan and be subject to previously agreed resources (human and material).

Termination of Association
6.0 This agreement may be terminated by either the OU's Academic Representative or the Editor of Didaskalia upon six months notice in writing. Full web-editing access would remain available until the termination of the six months notice. A web-page redirecting users to the subsequent location of Didaskalia would be maintained at the previous OU URL for Didaskalia for 12 months after the site had been removed from the OU server.

Ownership and Liability
7.0 The contents, assets and intellectual property of Didaskalia, current and future, will be the property of the Editor of Didaskalia. This will remain the case in the event that this agreement is terminated. Specific content or assets or intellectual property may be exempted from this provision if specified in writing in advance of their being made available to Didaskalia. Such exemptions may include materials made available as a result of collaborative projects, contributions by academic authors, or teaching and learning materials. All exemptions will be clearly noted wherever appropriate on the web-site.

7.1 The Open University will not be responsible for any costs or debts of Didaskalia outside the terms of this Agreement.

7.2 The Open University will not be responsible for any action regarding intellectual property, libel etc. brought against Didaskalia or its Editor(s).
