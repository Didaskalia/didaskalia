Phaedra by Yannis Ritsos
4th July, 2007
Camden People's Theatre in London, UK
Tickets: £7
To book call: 08700 600 100
Website: www.cptheatre.co.uk

Desideratum theatre presents Phaedra by Yannis Ritsos in association with the P.I. Network as part of the Groundwork5 Season at Camden People's Theatre. Directed by Christos Chanios, designed by Natasa Stamatari and performed by Maria Gaitanidi and Raymi Javier, Phaedra is a contemporary taking on the ancient myth in the form of a dramatic monologue, where the eponymous protagonist unravels images, anachronisms and memories from the recent and distant past, in an attempt to come to terms with her desire for her husband's son.

Helen by Euripides
23rd - 30th September, 2007
University of Utah, Salt Lake City, USA
Directed by Barbara Smith
Website: www.theatre.utah.edu/calendar_current/calendar.htm

In Euripides' surprising take on the tale, Helen was never in Troy, having been spirited away from Sparta and a fake Helen left in her place to be kidnapped and taken to Troy, thus touching off the 10-year war. Continuing Euripides' Trojan War plays, this year we uncover the fate of the real Helen, as she waits in Egypt for news of her husband and fends off a marriage to the new king. This production will tour to Weber State University, Westminster College, and Brigham Young University in September and October.
