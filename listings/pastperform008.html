Women of Troy by Euripides
16-20 November, 2004 Harborough Theatre, Market Harborough
A visiting production, Directed by Gill Lucchesi, Of Euripides' tragedy about how women are affected by war. Using the same set of warfield trenches just vacated by Journey's End (WW1 play by RC Sherriff) Women of Troy gives a picutre not just of the impact of invasion and destruction of the ancient city of Troy during the Trojan Wars, but of all wars across the centuries.

Hellenic Festival
18 June-September, 2004
The Hellenic Festival celebrates the 50 years since the birth of Epidaurus Festival ((1954 -2004) with 11 performances staged by 10 Greek and one Italian theatre company (Piccolo Teatro). Starting on June 18, this year's programme includes "The Trojan Women", a tribute to Aristophanes, "Bacchai", "Hippolytus", "Lysistrata", "Andromache", "Philoctetes", "Myrmidons, Nereids, Phryges", "Eumenides", "The Trachiniae" and "Wealth."

Parthenon Lost by Constantine Sandis
9 May, 2004 , 2.30pm , The Oxford Union, Frewin Court , Oxford , OX1 3JB
Directed by Constatine Sandis
A one-act playlet using the form of Socratic Dialogue to illuminate the moral and philosophical issues surrounding the question of returning the Parthenon Marbles to Greece . The play is accompanied by a debate, starting at 4.30pm , featuring Richard Allen MP; Peter Derow of Wadham College , Oxford ; Michael Daley of Art Watch; Boris Johnson MP and student debaters.
